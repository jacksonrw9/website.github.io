Sartorius Quintix
Data Connector

Enable secure, compliant data capture and transfer from the Sartorius Quintix analytical balance to your existing file servers. Eliminate manual data collection errors and streamline chain of custody with one simple validated solution.

Sartorius Quintix Data Connector Capabilities

  • Ensure Data Integrity by automating secure data collection and eliminate inefficiency and potential for human error from collection workflows
  • Send Validated Results securely to existing servers or backend systems without exposing your instruments to direct network threats
  • Protect Against Data Loss during network failures and power outages
  • Ensure Chain of Custody and Compliance with automated audit logging for each result

Extend the Lifespan of Legacy Instruments

Building Towards Industry 4.0 Standards

Rather than replacing your legacy instruments because of networking limitations or inadequate integration with modern digital systems, Phizzle's Sartorius Quintix Data Connector allows regulated manufacturers to modernize data collection practices with older existing instruments. This solution can either be the solution to analog data practices, or it can be the first step towards realizing Industry 4.0 Standards with the Phizzle Connector Plus.

Streamline Compliance and Chain of Custody

Uphold Chain of Custody by automatically collecting contextualized data for each record including: a sample ID, operator involved, time generated, location of the file and crc. The connector also enables operators to add notes to that file.

Frequently Asked Questions

Simply plug in the Phizzle Edge Puck via USB to the instrument and export data as if it were a regular flash drive. Data goes securely to our backend and our engineering team configures the data files to securely transfer to your server.

Our engineering team monitors firmware updates, but due to the simplicity of the data connector as long as the firmware doesn't eliminate the ability to export to USB or share to a file server there will be absolutely no change to the data connector's functionality.
